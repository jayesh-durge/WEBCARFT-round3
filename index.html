Understanding Global Warming

Global warming is a phenomenon where the Earth's temperature gradually increases due to human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels. This leads to various environmental impacts that threaten biodiversity, ecosystems, and human health.

Fossil Fuels

Burning coal, oil, and natural gas releases greenhouse gases, which are the largest contributors to global warming.

Deforestation

Forests play a crucial role in absorbing CO2, so cutting them down accelerates the greenhouse effect and climate change.

Industrial Pollution

Factories emit large amounts of greenhouse gases and pollutants, which contribute significantly to global warming.

Agriculture

Agricultural practices, particularly livestock farming, produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to warming.
